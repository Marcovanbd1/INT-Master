What it is

Neighbourhood Signals is a way for a place to notice what people are trying to find help with, and how that shifts week to week, using aggregate digital behaviour as a clue.

It’s not a patient list. It’s not risk scoring. It’s not a surveillance product. It’s a shared civic picture of emerging pressure, held with humility and used for gentle coordination.

The loop

Signals only works safely when the loop is closed.

Listen
→
Understand
→
Interpret
→
Respond
“Respond” here means small, reversible actions that reduce confusion and improve access. Not targeting. Not case management. Not behavioural nudging.

The 30 day listening sprint

This is not a procurement and not a platform rollout. It’s a lightweight, timeboxed way to practise listening together, then publishing what we heard and what we did.

No integration. No personal data. No profiling. Just shared interpretation and gentle response.

This doesn’t scale, and it’s not a roll-out

Growth is not replication. Neighbourhoods don’t sound the same. So new areas don’t “adopt a tool”, they commission stewardship and grow their own listening loop safely.
